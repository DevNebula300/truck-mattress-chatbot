# Estee Bedding – General FAQs

## How often should I replace my mattress?

Every **5–7 years.** Even if your mattress doesn't show much wear and tear, your body, lifestyle, and sleep needs have probably changed. If your mattress is 5–7 years older, so are you — has your weight changed, have you gotten married, developed back problems, or do you have a less active lifestyle? That old mattress may no longer be the best fit for the person you are today.

## If I have a bad back, should I buy a hard mattress?

Your doctor may have specific recommendations for your sleep surface. In general, we recommend a mattress that offers **good support**, and support does **not** mean the hardest or firmest mattress available. Innerspring mattresses are available in various types of surface materials over the coil unit that help improve comfort without compromising support. Foam, latex, or air mattresses are also good options for contouring the body while offering proper support and lasting comfort.

## What are the regulations on mattresses being fire resistant?

Beginning **July 1st, 2007**, all mattresses manufactured must meet the federal flammability regulations (F.R.). This law requires that every mattress have a **label** stating the mattress set meets the federal standard. Mattresses must be significantly more resistant to an open flame than previously outlined in individual state rulings. **All Estee Bedding mattresses** meet these flammability regulations by means of **fire-resistant fiber barriers**, not with hazardous chemicals. The regulation is designed so that in case of a fire, occupants have an extra **20–30 minutes** to escape the bedroom and firefighters have more time to respond. It is estimated this law saves about **270 lives** each year and prevents as many as **1,330 injuries.**
